## Cool Hawaii stats (verified — for blog credibility, intros & "borrowed authority")

Real, citable facts to sprinkle into posts (great for openers and the Kennedy "proof-with-specifics"
device). **Stable facts** are safe to state; **figures that change** (visitor counts, rainfall, seal
population) should **link the official source** and be checked current. Never state one not on a list
like this without a source.

**Geography & nature**
- Hawaii is the **most remote major population center on Earth** — about **2,400 miles** from the US
  mainland. *(widely documented)*
- The **only US state made entirely of islands** — 137 in the chain, **8 main islands**; also the only
  state outside North America and the **southernmost** US state (Ka Lae / South Point, Big Island).
- **Mauna Kea is the tallest mountain on Earth base-to-summit** (~33,500 ft from the ocean floor);
  summit **13,803 ft**. **Mauna Loa** is the largest active volcano on Earth by volume. *(USGS / UH)*
- **Kīlauea** is one of the world's **most active volcanoes**; the islands sit over a hotspot and the
  Pacific Plate creeps ~**3–4 inches/year**, so Hawaii is literally still growing. *(USGS)*
- **Hilo** is one of the **wettest cities in the US** (~**126–130 inches** of rain a year). *(NWS — verify current)*
- All Hawaii **beaches are public by law**, even those fronting resorts. *(Hawaii shoreline access law)*

**Culture & people**
- Hawaii has **two official languages**: English and **ʻŌlelo Hawaiʻi** (Hawaiian). *(state constitution)*
- The **Hawaiian alphabet has 13 letters** — 5 vowels, 8 consonants (including the ʻokina).
- **Surfing (heʻe nalu) originated in Hawaii**, the "sport of kings."
- The **shaka is Hawaii's official state gesture** (Act 85, 2024). *(HRS §5-23)*
- State fish: the **humuhumunukunukuāpuaʻa**.
- Hawaii keeps its **own time zone (HST)** and does **not** observe daylight saving time.
- Hawaii eats **more Spam per capita** than any US state (~5–7 million cans/yr). *(widely reported)*

**Wildlife & environment**
- The **Hawaiian monk seal** is one of the world's most endangered seals — only about **1,500** remain.
  *(NOAA — verify current)*
- Green sea turtles (**honu**) and monk seals are **protected** — stay 10+ ft back (it's the law).
- Hawaii is the **endemism capital of the US** — a huge share of its native species exist nowhere else
  (and, sadly, it's also called the "extinction capital"). *(USFWS / Bishop Museum — verify specifics)*

**Visitors & economy**
- Hawaii hosts roughly **9–10 million visitors a year**. *(Hawaii Tourism Authority / DBEDT — link + verify current)*
