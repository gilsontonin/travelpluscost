# Writing Lessons — the feedback loop

Mistakes caught in **review** become rules at **write** time, so we stop repeating them.
`review-blog` appends a one-line lesson here when it finds a *systemic* miss (not a one-off);
`write-blog-post` and `create-money-page` read this list before drafting. Keep each lesson a
single, actionable line. Prune lessons that are now fully enforced by a checker.

## Lessons

- **COPY SURFER'S SPEC — don't deviate, don't reinvent past it.** Surfer is the proven industry standard;
  mirror it. MATCH their published targets: **word count (write to the competitor median — NEVER a shorter
  "helpful" version), paragraph count, heading count, every term's range (fill unders, trim overs), and put
  the exact phrase + every recommended subtopic in an H2/H3.** Never ship under the competitor MEDIAN to be
  concise — that single deviation tanks the score. *(maui-itinerary shipped at 2,310 words vs Surfer's
  5.7-6.5K target → SEO 47/AI 52; redone to 5,685 = matched spec. 2026-06-10)* **But "match the median" is a
  BAND with a ceiling, not "go as long as possible": Surfer's word target is a range and it EXCLUDES the
  bloated outlier — over the longest genuine competitor is the overshoot bug (see the band lesson below).**

- **TL;DR points must be 3–5 real takeaways, not section titles.** Each is a bold-led, one-line
  payoff a skimmer can act on (e.g. "**Manta night snorkel** — the one unmissable thing") — never a
  ToC label like "What's in this guide." *(kona review, 2026-06)*
- **Cover the SERP's sub-intents, not just the headline keyword.** Skim the live top 3 and include
  the sub-topics they all have — e.g. a "things to do in X" post needs a **luau** section if the
  competitors feature one. A gap there is a ranking gap. *(kona review, 2026-06)*
- **Every `::tour` / `tours[]` code must be the RIGHT ISLAND.** Cached Viator codes for the wrong
  destination exist (an Aruba and a Florida snorkel tour were live on an Oahu post). Confirm the code's
  Viator URL path is `/tours/<correct-island>/` before using it. *(mermaid-cave audit, 2026-06)*
- **Link the newer hubs.** Older posts predate `/things-to-do/` and `/where-to-stay/` — add a relevant
  hub link when a post discusses tours or lodging. *(kona review, 2026-06)*
- **Length is SERP-driven, never a fixed number.** Match within ~20% of the live top-3 for the keyword;
  thin monthly-search keywords get short posts. Padding to hit a word count is an AI-tell. *(2026-06)*
- **Length = the serp-optimize BAND, and overshoot is as wrong as undershoot.** The standard is the
  Surfer-calibrated band: floor = competitor MEDIAN, working target = median×1.15, ceiling = the longest
  GENUINE competitor; a competitor ≥1.5× the median is an OUTLIER and is EXCLUDED. **"Never shorter than #1
  genuine" was WRONG** — it re-imported the very outlier the band drops, and combined with a fixed "10–13 H2s
  × ≥250w/section" floor it shipped EVERY recent post at ~2–2.5× the band (keawakapu: band 1,491–1,759, shipped
  4,407). Fix: **section COUNT = working-target ÷ ~280** (so a ~1,700 band → ~6–8 H2s, not 12–14), and once OVER
  the ceiling MERGE/CUT, never deepen. `post-stats.mjs` now auto-loads the band from the brief and flags both
  ends; `serp-optimize.mjs` prints `Target band: lo-hi`. *(post-stats overshoot fix, 2026-06-18)*
- **Internal linking is bidirectional.** A new post links OUT to 3–5 relevant posts AND earns backlinks
  FROM the older posts it now best-answers (replace weaker link targets with it). *(2026-06)*
- **Author/byline must be the current brand.** It's **Hawaii Picnics & Beach Events** — the `author`
  field renders in the visible byline, so it must never say the legacy "Hawaii Picnics by Wember".
  (All 93 existing posts still carry the old name — flagged for a separate global fix.) *(dole-plantation review, 2026-06)*
- **Prefer Unsplash/self-hosted images over Pexels.** Lighthouse's `third-party-cookies` audit now
  tanks **best-practices** to ~77 on any post that loads `images.pexels.com` (the Pexels CDN sets
  third-party cookies). Perf/SEO/a11y are unaffected; best-practices only recovers once the image is
  off Pexels — another reason to finish the Unsplash migration. *(dole-plantation review, 2026-06)*

- **Bold the ANSWER PHRASE, not the whole sentence** — in the intro (the primary-keyword answer) and at the start of every FAQ answer. Bolding a full sentence is a recurring slip. *(garlic-shrimp self-audit, 2026-06)*
- **Add a "quick facts" strip per spot/item** (Cost · Drive/Time · Cash? · The move) — a scannable bold-led line or a stat-panel. Keeps getting skipped. *(garlic-shrimp self-audit, 2026-06)*
- **Any X-vs-Y is a compare VISUAL, not just prose** — Giovanni's vs Romy's shipped as prose; should have been a compare card. *(garlic-shrimp self-audit, 2026-06)*
- **FAQ holds LEFTOVER questions only** — never re-answer a question the body already covers. *(manapua self-audit, 2026-06)*
- **Run BlogPostChecklist.md line-by-line as a hard gate** — spot-checking lets misses through; tick every box and report the table. *(garlic-shrimp self-audit, 2026-06)*
- **Re-run `post-stats.mjs` AFTER deepening sections, and pass its VISUALS check on the FINAL word count — not the first pass.** The 1-infographic-per-~500-words target *scales with length*, so fattening thin sections can silently drop you below cadence (a post that passed at 6 visuals/3,120w needed 7 at 3,400w). The visual cadence is satisfied by NATIVE HTML infographics (cards/stat-panel/compare/steps/graph) — don't lean on the tour card + directions to hit the number. *(beaches-in-honolulu self-audit, 2026-06)*
- **Okina/apostrophe place names create PHANTOM term gaps — log them as residuals, never chase them.** The scorer DELETES okinas and apostrophes from our text ("Papaʻiloa"/"Papa'iloa" → one token `papailoa`), but competitor HTML often carries the okina as an entity that the fetcher converts to a SPACE → their corpus yields `papa iloa` / `papa iloa beach` as "missing" heading+body terms we can never match without misspelling the name. Sibling of the hyphenation lesson. *(papailoa review, 2026-06-11 — capped heading at 67%, owner waived at 73)*
- **Hyphenated phrasings don't count toward term ranges.** The scorer's tokenizer keeps hyphens, so "Size-wise"/"ocean-front"/"yellow-orange" never match the terms `size`/`ocean`/`orange`. When the brief flags a term you're sure you wrote, check for a hyphen — and prefer the unhyphenated phrasing where grammar allows. *(hawaii-state-fish review, 2026-06-12)* **Corollary: a HYPHENATED PRIMARY KEYWORD can never pass the exact-phrase check** — the scorer strips the keyword's hyphen but not the body's, so "byodo-in temple oahu" is structurally 0× even when the phrase is in the post; treat the missing 15 pts as an artifact residual, not a gap. *(byodo-in review, 2026-06-11 — waived at 82 with body 94%/heading 100%)*
- **Converting prose into a native infographic DELETES its terms from the scored body** — the scorer reads only the markdown body, not `infographics.ts`. When a list/paragraph full of entities (film titles, place names) becomes cards, keep ONE entity-rich prose sentence beside the visual or the body score silently drops (halona's film entities all went to 0 after the cards conversion). Complement, don't duplicate: names in prose, details in cards. *(halona review, 2026-06-11)*
- **COMMAS inside the primary keyword break the exact-phrase check — write it comma-free once.** The check is a literal substring match, so "Rainbow Falls, Big Island, Hawaii" scores 0× for `rainbow falls big island hawaii`. For multi-part place keywords, frame one mention as a search string ('type "Rainbow Falls Big Island Hawaii" into Google Maps') — natural, comma-free, and how people actually search. Sibling of the hyphenated-keyword lesson. *(rainbow-falls review, 2026-06-11)*
- **An in-body image that duplicates native infographics is an LCP thief — make it native or cut it.** The first in-body image loads eager + `fetchpriority=high`; below the fold it steals critical bandwidth from the font/CSS the real LCP (TL;DR text) needs. best-sunset's only in-body image was an image-infographic duplicating 3 native panels — removing it (and covering the content natively) lifted perf 90→95. Audit other posts using the image-infographic path. *(best-sunset review, 2026-06-11)*
- **Fact-check infographics AGAINST the body — they're authored separately and drift.** china-walls' body correctly said winter=calm/summer=south-swell, while its `china-walls-when` infographic said the opposite (copied from competitors' shared error) and `china-walls-facts` claimed 60–70 ft ledges (that's Spitting Cave; the jump is 5–15 ft). When reviewing or writing, read every `::infographic`'s definition in `content/infographics.ts` next to the section it sits in. *(china-walls review, 2026-06-11)*
- **"Rewrite in the new style" = research-first, not presentation-first.** Re-pull the live SERP (top genuine competitors, length, current PAA), find the gap and add what competitors MISS, verify the body answers the SERP questions — THEN do the visual/formatting layer. A refresh that only swaps photos + bolding is half the job. *(best-time self-audit, 2026-06)*
- **Review runs must audit the POST FIELDS, not just the body** — `tldr` (real takeaways, not section titles, answer ≠ excerpt dupe), `tours[]` bottom block, cover-image host, stale comments. The scorer reads only the markdown body, so field rot is invisible to every gate except a human field check; maui review #1 shipped with 2019-era tldr section-titles + no tours block until the owner asked "did you finish properly." *(things-to-do-in-maui, 2026-06-12)*
- **A numbered list is ONE paragraph to the 150-word cap (checklist 6.4)** — the checker exempts `-` bullets and `|` tables but not `1.` lists, so a 5-step recipe list trips it. Keep numbered lists ≤150 words total: tight imperative steps, with the detail in surrounding prose or an H3 (where the scorer reads it anyway). *(spam-musubi review, 2026-06-12)*
- **Body markdown must start at column 0.** `post-stats.mjs` and the scorer split sections on `\n## ` — an indented body parses as 0 H2s and every per-section gate goes blind. Match the 1,500+ existing column-0 headings, not the handful of legacy indented bodies. *(north-shore-oahu-restaurants run, 2026-06-13)*
- **Check the nearest sibling post's cover + infographics BEFORE choosing yours.** The NS-restaurants run nearly duplicated best-places-to-eat-oahu's exact cover photo AND its Giovanni's-vs-Romy's compare card — a sibling echo reads as thin content. Grep blog.ts for the cover photo-ID and skim the sibling's `::infographic` keys first; pick fresh angles. *(north-shore-oahu-restaurants run, 2026-06-13)*
- **On a thin single-property SERP, the HEADING lever (×0.35) clears 90 without body-gutting — and the scorer's heading check is a RAW `.includes()`, so `&`/`and` break a phrase.** When stuck in the low-to-mid 80s because every remaining body lever is an artifact TRIM (e.g. `beach ≤25` on a beach resort, `kaanapali ≤8` on a Kaanapali hotel, `water ≤2` on a snorkeling review) and the only ADD terms left are off-topic rival hotels, push HEADING coverage instead — it's the biggest weight (×0.35) and needs no on-topic word removed. CRITICAL mechanism: `inDraftHeading()` matches the term as a raw lowercased substring of the heading text (not tokenized), so a heading reading "Resort & Spa" or "Resort and Spa" FAILS `.includes("resort spa")` — the `&`/`and` sits between the words. Put the bare contiguous phrase ("Sheraton Maui Resort Spa") in ≥1 heading and you credit `resort spa` + `maui resort spa` + `sheraton maui resort spa` at once. Slugify strips `&`, so the heading id (and ToC anchor) is unchanged either way. *(sheraton-maui review, 2026-06-13 — owner call took 84→93: heading 75%→100%, zero body edits)*
- **The dehyphenate step must NEVER touch machine-read tokens — `::infographic <key>` and `](#anchor)` fragments.** Both are hyphen-joined by design (the render regex is `/[a-z0-9-]+/`; heading ids slugify spaces→hyphens), so despacing them silently breaks EVERY infographic (leaks as literal `::infographic …` text) and EVERY ToC link (href no longer matches the id). A bad run did exactly this to 382 keys + 1,480 anchors across 147 posts. `dehyphenate.mjs` now guards them (guardL excludes `#`; transform skips `::` lines incl. indented/escaped-`\n`), but VERIFY after any prose-hyphen pass: built HTML must have 0 literal `::infographic` and 0 `href="#x y"`. *(dehyphenation regression, 2026-06-13)*
- **The checklist parses literally: the FAQ H2 must START with "## FAQ" and the H1 title must contain the keyword's last two words CONTIGUOUSLY.** "Road to Hana Maui FAQ" → 0 FAQ answers detected (3.5 fails); "…Hana: The Ultimate Maui…" → 2.3 fails because punctuation breaks the "hana maui" substring. Carry a keyword into the FAQ heading as "FAQ: <keyword>", and keep the keyword's tail unbroken in the title. *(road-to-hana review, 2026-06-12)*
- **A RESORT/HOTEL ROUND-UP needs resort-NAME H3 subheadings — a thematic structure starves the heading lever (×0.35).** On `maui-all-inclusive-resorts` I structured the post thematically (why none / what's included / fake it / what to book) and scored **48** with heading at **9%**, because every competitor is a listicle whose headings are property names (Grand Wailea, Wailea Beach Resort, Hana-Maui, Royal Lahaina). Fix: convert the resort discussion into per-property **H3s** (`### Grand Wailea…`, `### Fairmont Kea Lani`, `### Wailea Beach Resort, Marriott`, `### Royal Lahaina Resort`) + one "big extras" H3 carrying `resort spa` — heading **9% → 100%**, score **48 → 90**, with genuinely better structure. For any "best X resorts/hotels/restaurants in Y" round-up, give each named entity its own H3 from the start; the exemplars (best-places-to-eat-oahu, north-shore-oahu-restaurants) already do. *(maui-all-inclusive run, 2026-06-13)*
- **A HYPHENATED BRAND name + a SPACED keyword = the dual-form trap (Ritz-Carlton edition).** The keyword "ritz carlton maui reviews" is spaced, but the brand "Ritz-Carlton" is hyphenated — and `ritz-carlton` is in dehyphenate KEEP, so the prose keeps the hyphen and "ritz-carlton maui reviews" never matches the spaced keyword's exact-phrase check. Fix (same as the all-inclusive lesson): write the SPACED form ("Ritz Carlton Maui reviews") in the intro first-100-words and the §8 worth-it heading for the exact-phrase (landed 4×), and keep the HYPHENATED "Ritz-Carlton" everywhere else (it's the dominant competitor body term and scores `ritz-carlton`). Dehyphenate leaves both alone (it only removes hyphens; the spaced form has none, the hyphenated form is KEPT). Note 2.3 passes off the last-two-words "maui reviews," which is comma-free in the title regardless of the brand hyphen. *(ritz-carlton-kapalua review, 2026-06-13)*
- **A sacred or sensitive history, handled with full reverence, is the engagement CORE — not a paragraph to skip.** The Ritz-Carlton Kapalua's defining story is that it was moved inland in 1987 to protect the Honokahua burial site (900+ iwi kupuna), which helped pass Hawaii's 1990 burial-protection law. Every generic competitor review buries this; leading with it (its own H2 + a respectful timeline infographic) is the single most distinctive, most-quoted, most-trust-building thing in the post — exactly the "Experience" signal Google rewards and the reason a reader stays. Rule: when a property has a real cultural/historical weight, make it a section, verify it across sources, and write it with zero humour anywhere near it (somber-topic rule). *(ritz-carlton-kapalua review, 2026-06-13)*
- **The COMMA trap isn't just the serp exact-phrase — it also fails checklist 2.3 (keyword in H1 + first 100 words), and BOLD MARKERS break it too.** A keyword whose natural rendering carries a comma ("Montage Kapalua Bay**,** Maui") is broken in three places at once: the serp exact-phrase substring match (0×), the checklist's H1/title check, and the first-100-words check — because the comma sits between "bay" and "maui." Worse, bolding part of it (`**Montage Kapalua Bay** Maui`) inserts `**` between the words and breaks the contiguity the same way. Fix: put a comma-free, marker-free contiguous form ("Montage Kapalua Bay Maui") in the H1 title, in the intro's first 100 words, and in ≥1 heading — bold the *answer phrase* separately, not the keyword. Sibling of the rainbow-falls comma lesson and the byodo-in hyphen lesson. *(montage-kapalua-bay review, 2026-06-13)*
- **A property mid-REBRAND is a freshness goldmine — lead with "it's not X anymore."** Montage Kapalua Bay became Marriott's The Resort at Kapalua Bay in March 2026 (→ St. Regis 2027), so every competitor review still calling it "the Montage" is stale. Targeting the old (still-searched) brand name while delivering the honest current answer is the same answer-first move as Honua Kai's "it's not a hotel" — and exactly what Google's freshness + Experience signals reward. When picking a property spoke, a recent rebrand/closure/renovation is a feature, not a reason to avoid it. *(montage-kapalua-bay review, 2026-06-13)*
- **At the artifact ceiling (heading 100%, all scored terms filled, but sub-90 from OVER-penalty), trim the ToC LINK TEXT — not the headings.** The serp scorer reads the whole markdown body, so the ToC list (`- [Honua Kai Resort Spa vs the Kaanapali hotels](#anchor)`) DUPLICATES every property phrase already in its heading, double-counting it in the BODY frequency and tipping high-value phrases into "↑ over" (the scorer penalizes stuffing). On `honua-kai-resort-maui` the heading-lever pushed "kai resort spa" to 8× (4 headings + 4 ToC link-texts) vs a ≤6 ceiling. Fix: shorten the ToC DISPLAY text (`[Honua Kai vs the Kaanapali hotels](#honua-kai-resort-spa-vs-the-kaanapali-hotels)`) — the `#anchor` and the H2 are untouched, so heading coverage stays 100% while the body over-count drops back into range. Took 89→90 with zero heading-coverage loss. The companion move to the heading lever: the lever ADDS phrases to headings; this REMOVES their ToC echo when they overshoot. *(honua-kai-resort-maui run, 2026-06-13)*
- **Bedroom-count compounds (`one/two/three/four-bedroom`) are competitor-hyphenated terms — KEEP them in dehyphenate.** The dehyphenate house-style pass flattened "three-bedroom"→"three bedroom" and dropped a 67% scored term (90→88). Added `one/two/three/four-bedroom` to `dehyphenate.mjs` KEEP (sibling of the `adults-only`/`all-inclusive` fix) and re-hyphenated. Note the KEEP key is exact: "three-bedroom" (singular) is protected, "three-bedrooms" (plural) is NOT — write the singular adjective form where the term matters. *(honua-kai-resort-maui run, 2026-06-13)*
- **Hyphenated SPACED keyword + hyphenated prose adjective = use BOTH forms.** Keyword "maui all inclusive resorts" is spaced, but the natural adjective is "all-inclusive" (hyphenated — the 100% competitor body term; scorer keeps hyphens). Write the SPACED form in title/H1/headings/FAQ (so the exact-phrase substring check passes) and the HYPHENATED form as the prose adjective (so the body term scores), and add the hyphenated compound to `dehyphenate.mjs` KEEP so the prose-hyphen pass doesn't strip it. Sibling of the byodo-in hyphenated-keyword lesson and the adults-only KEEP fix. *(maui-all-inclusive run, 2026-06-13)*
- **`family-friendly` and `kid-friendly` must be in dehyphenate KEEP — they're aim competitor terms.** Both are hyphenated adjectives the scorer treats as single tokens (it keeps hyphens), and `family-friendly` is a 100%/high-aim competitor body term on any family-lodging SERP. The dehyphenate house-style pass would flatten them to "family friendly"/"kid friendly" and drop the term matches. Added `family-friendly kid-friendly` to `dehyphenate.mjs` KEEP (sibling of adults-only/all-inclusive/bedroom-count). *(maui-family-resorts run, 2026-06-13)*
- **A CAPSTONE round-up of properties you've already reviewed is the topical-authority hub move — and the heading-lever scales to it.** After building ~9 single-property Maui reviews, the "maui family resorts" round-up links OUT to every one of them (grouped South vs West) — that's the session-depth/engagement play (a reader who clicks "read the full X review" is a two-page session) AND the cluster's authority capstone; the spokes then link back IN (3 backlinks: the where-to-stay hub's "Families" line, the condo review's "families come back" line, the kid-resort's "book it if you have kids" verdict). Score path 72→79→90→93: body-lift the kids'-club/activities vocabulary the infographics swallowed (activities/kids clubs/water slides/children/ocean — Playbook rule 11), then the HEADING lever — per-property H3s + the contiguous "Resort Spa" form (drop the &/and, matching competitor headings) in 4 H3s + a renamed picks H2 carrying "maui resorts" + one genuinely-useful "Montage Kapalua Bay (now The Resort at Kapalua Bay)" H3 (covers the montage-kapalua-bay/kapalua-bay heading cluster AND is a real multigen option) took heading 76%→100% with zero body-gutting. *(maui-family-resorts run, 2026-06-13)*
- **An APOSTROPHE/okina primary keyword fails checklist 2.3's literal `first100.includes(kw)` — pass the ASCII form to `--kw`.** Checklist 2.3 does a raw `body.toLowerCase().includes(kw)`, and our content is ASCII per rule 3a, so the source keyword "ali'i kula lavender farm maui" (straight apostrophe) is never a substring of our "alii kula lavender farm maui" body → false AUTO fail. The serp scorer already strips the apostrophe and our title/H1/intro/heading all carry the contiguous ASCII form, so the keyword genuinely IS present — run `node scripts/post-checklist.mjs <slug> --kw "alii kula lavender farm maui"` (no apostrophe), the canonical rendered form. Sibling of the okina/papailoa term-gap + the rainbow-falls/montage comma traps. *(alii-kula-lavender-farm-maui run, 2026-06-15)*

- **WRITE FUNNY FIRST — a true statement is not a joke (the dry-reading miss).** The recurring failure is info-first-then-sprinkle: write the encyclopedia, drop a mild aside on top → decoration, not a beat. Draft the joke INTO the section so it carries the fact (swap-meet: "keeps banker's hours, if the bank only opened on Saturday and shut before lunch" *is* the hours). Target voice = a friend at a bar dropping truths, a little sarcastic, not taking life too seriously, riding the vacation mood — respectful, never mean. **The on-point test for every beat: does it earn a nose-exhale/nod, or is it merely accurate?** If only accurate, sharpen (specific / absurd-but-true / stakes-inflation) or cut. Aim ~1–2 real beats per non-somber section; zero near somber topics (drownings, the Lahaina fire). Full guide: `References/Humour.md`. *(best-snorkeling-in-maui + black-sand-beach-maui shipped DRY, owner-corrected 2026-06-16)*

### travelpluscost — "where to stay in <city>" program (inventory-forward, real LiteAPI/directory data)

- **THE DEPLOY GATE IS `npm run check`, not just build/lint.** Netlify runs `npm run sitemaps && npm run build && npm run check`, and a RED `check` (claims-integrity + ai-slop) FAILS THE WHOLE DEPLOY — so a NEW-slug post 404s on prod while stale content keeps serving (looks like "only this one post is missing"). Run `npm run check` before every push. It rejects unverifiable superlatives: **"most-reviewed" (×4) silently blocked every deploy** until rephrased to verifiable forms (review counts / "busiest" / "a local favorite"); "best-reviewed" was NOT flagged. *(moab + bend, 2026-06-24)*
- **One push at a time; let the Netlify build FINISH.** Rapid back-to-back "go live" pushes supersede builds, and a new slug then 404s even though the code is correct; the fix is Netlify → Deploys → **Clear cache and deploy site**. A single clean push (deploy gate green) published the new Bend slug in ~100s with no clear-cache. *(moab → bend, 2026-06-24)*
- **`blog:voice` CONCISE over-counts: decimals + "Mt." are sentence-ends, and blank-line-free lines merge into ONE paragraph.** A body BULLET LIST is read as a single paragraph and trips the ">3 sentences" HARD floor — write skimmable content (e.g. "what to do") as PROSE, not bullets. A verdict with 3 sentences + two score-decimals (two "9.8"s) also falsely flags — keep score-bearing paragraphs to ≤2 real sentences, and prefer "Mount" over "Mt." (the counter strips St./Dr./Ms./Hwy but NOT Mt.). *(bend, 2026-06-24 — cost ~6 gate loops)*
- **Pair the head term with the STATE to disambiguate AND add volume.** "where to stay in bend oregon" (480) beats bare "where to stay in bend" (260) and dodges Big Bend TX / South Bend IN — check `phrase_fullsearch`, put the winning pairing in the title/H1 and 4–5 H2s (the heading lever ×0.35). Sibling of the Estes Park "Colorado" pairing. *(bend, 2026-06-24)*
- **A "hotels side by side" RECAP TABLE triggers a cta-audit "mentioned-hotel gap" for every row** — but they're all carded above, so it's a false positive (0 real gaps, 0 leaks). Reason and keep it; don't link-spam the rows to silence the audit. *(bend, 2026-06-24)*
- **A high-volume DESTINATION town is the opposite problem: a COMPREHENSIVE-COMPETITOR SERP where Body is LENGTH-gated.** Sedona (vol 2,900, KD 24) ranks behind 3,300–4,800-word personal mega-guides ("from someone who's been 30 times"), so even with the heading lever maxed (100%) and every real entity woven, Body sits ~50% at ~2,500w and serp caps ~75. Build the genuinely comprehensive guide (here: 16 cards across 4 areas + a what-to-do, a getting-around/Red Rock Pass, a how-many-days/when-to-go section, 8 FAQs, 2 infographics) — but the last ~800w to the floor is competitor padding (pros/cons, price tiers, "check availability" CTAs), so report serp + the under-floor length as a comprehensive-competitor EXEC-DECISION rather than pad it. Same family as Oahu's entity-density ceiling, just higher because heading maxed. Vet these on volume first — the bigger volume justifies the bigger build and the lower ceiling. *(sedona 75, 2026-06-24)*
- **Hotel SUB-BRAND names can be HARD AI-slop tells — "Tapestry" (Hilton's Tapestry Collection) flags `tapestry`.** The slop checker doesn't know it's a brand; rephrase ("a boutique hotel" / "a modern hotel"), which often scores a wanted term anyway. Sibling of the okina/place-name traps but for brands. *(sedona, 2026-06-24)*
- **The cover for a red-rock/scenery town is the famous PROPERTY's best red-rock photo, not a town shot.** Enchantment Resort's adobe-casitas-under-the-cliffs-at-dusk is unmistakably Sedona and one we can price; a hotel's entrance-sign photo (Sky Ranch Lodge) is not a cover. Inspect 3–4 candidates, pick the one that screams the destination. *(sedona, 2026-06-24)*
