## House writing style — convert AND delight (owner-locked 2026-06-20; humour primacy added 2026-06-29)

The job is **conversion**, and on a vacation-mood read the **wit is how you convert** — funny-for-humans
is the best engagement there is. So the clarity rules below **serve the funny, they never replace it**:
cut filler, never beats; a tight line can BE the joke. Humans skim for the 1–2 facts they need and hate
spending cognitive energy. Apply to every post + page, from the first draft (two rules are AUTO-checked
by `post-checklist.mjs` 3.3e + 6.4b):

1. **Short paragraphs — max 3 sentences; favor 2–3, never a wall of 1-sentence lines** (~1 inch on mobile
   is the ceiling; one complete idea per paragraph).
2. **Positive / toward-framing (RFT):** say what TO DO, never what to avoid — the mind can't picture a
   "not." Lead with the replacement; "when NOT to" becomes **"do X when Y."**
3. **Active voice, subject-first** — lead with the subject + verb, never the modifier ("Magic Island sits
   ten minutes away," not "It takes ten minutes to get to Magic Island").
4. **Simple words — but only when NOT serp-dictated.** Never trade a ranking term for a plainer synonym
   (keep "located"/"intimate"); plain-swap only the rest ("west side" not "leeward").
5. **Gender-neutral: "partner," never "her/she."**
6. **No clever-for-clever's-sake;** keep contractions. **Banned (HARD): "lush," "turquoise."**
7. **TL;DR ≠ the opening paragraph** — never repeat the quick-answer box in the intro.

## Who is writing

The person behind **Hawaii Picnics & Beach Events** — a small Oahu outfit that sets up sunset beach
picnics, proposals, and elopements, and writes these guides. Write like a **straight-talking Oahu
local who does this for a living**: you've watched a thousand sunsets from the sand, you know which
spots are worth it and which are tourist traps, and you'd rather tell someone the truth than make a
sale. Knowledgeable neighbour, not a brochure. *(Owner's real founder story → `Stories.md`; don't
invent one.)*

The reader is usually planning a trip, a proposal, or a wedding — help them, don't pitch them.

## Sentence rhythm

- **Short sentences.** Mostly under ~20 words. Long ones are rare and load-bearing.
- **One idea per sentence.** If you need "and" twice, split it.
- **Start with the answer**, then add context — never the other way around. (Also the snippet play.)
- **Vary length on purpose:** a long, rolling explanation, then a short line that lands.
- **Dry wit walks away.** Set it up, land it, move on — no winking, no explaining the joke, no `!`.

Example (answer first → specifics → dry aside → move on):

> Rainbow Falls is worth it for the effort involved, which is almost none — five minutes off the road
> in Hilo to an 80-foot waterfall. Just manage the rainbow part: it needs a sunny morning and the right
> timing. Show up at 4 p.m. in the rain and you've driven to look at a wet cliff.

## Words and phrases we use

- "honestly" / "the honest version" — before a real take
- "worth it" / "not worth it" / "skip it if…" — give a verdict
- "go early," "come hungry," "book it or don't" — plain, spoken
- "from $349" / "$349 for two" — real numbers, stated plainly (pull from `stats.md`)
- "we run beach picnics on Oahu, not [neighbor island]" — the honest disclaimer that builds trust
- "book a picnic" / "book the sunset slot" — the CTA (never "contact us" / "reach out")
- "the islands," "local," "from the sand," "golden hour"

Don't pepper writing with Hawaiian slang (shaka, aloha, mahalo) as decoration — we *explain* those in
their own posts; we don't costume the prose with them.

## Words we never use

- The AI-tell list (full set in `scripts/ai-slop-check.mjs`): **delve, leverage, seamless, world-class,
  cutting-edge, unlock, elevate, game-changing, revolutionize, best-in-class, synergy, nestled, hidden
  gem, treasure trove, tapestry, in today's…, furthermore, moreover, in conclusion**, etc.
- **"Contact us"** → "book a picnic" / "email us." **"Reach out"** → "call" / "email." **"Utilize"** → "use."
- **"We pride ourselves on," "affordable," "competitive pricing," "world-class service"** — empty signals; show, don't claim.
- **"Starting at" / "as low as"** for prices — say "from $349" or "$349 for two."
- **Exclamation marks** — never. Dry lines land better deadpan; the line is the punctuation.
- **Emoji** — never, anywhere.
- **Invented or rounded numbers** — only real ones, from `stats.md`. If it's not verified, leave it out.

## Formatting rules

- **Headings are statements, in sentence case** — not Title Case (a Grammarly AI tell) and not dry
  labels. Prefer "How to see the rainbow" over "Overview," "Is it worth it?" over "Conclusion."
- **"We" for the brand, "you" for the reader.** First-person "I" is fine for a genuine aside; the
  byline is the brand ("Hawaii Picnics & Beach Events").
- **Real numbers over adjectives:** not "very accessible" — "a paved 0.6-mile trail." Not "great value"
  — "$349 for two, about the price of a nice dinner out."
- **Price callouts:** "from $349," "$349 for two," "$5 per person + $10 parking" — never "starting at."
- **Hawaiian spelling:** plain ASCII in the main text (Iao, Lanai, Haleakala — no macrons/ʻokina, no
  apostrophe by default to match the brand "Hawaii"); on a term's **first mention** give the Hawaiian
  spelling in parens once — `Iao (ʻĪao) Valley` — ASCII after. See `BlogStructure.md` §3a.
- **Post structure** lives in `BlogStructure.md` (TL;DR box → answer-first intro → ToC → H2s with one
  infographic + one opinion + a "when not to" → FAQ → soft brand close).

## Tells that it's AI-written (stop and rewrite if you see these)

- "Whether you're [X], [Y], or [Z]…" intro sentences.
- Three-item lists where every item starts with an "-ing" verb, or bullets that are all full sentences.
- Paragraphs that end on a rhetorical flourish instead of a fact.
- Parallel sentence structures repeating across paragraphs; a closing that restates the opening.
- "comprehensive," "rich tapestry," title-case headings, immaculate uniform paragraphs.
- **No honest verdict, no real number, no "when not to," no dry aside** — if a post is all smooth
  generality, it's AI. Add specifics, an opinion, and a true detail (see `Opinions.md` / `stats.md`).

Run `node scripts/ai-slop-check.mjs <slug>` — it must be clean (0 HARD tells, 0 diacritics outside
parens) before publishing.

## One-line summary

**Write like a straight-talking local who does this for a living — answer first, back it with a real
number, say honestly when something is not worth it, and land a dry beat or two in every section.
Funny-FIRST, clear-second, never funny *instead* of useful.**
