# Voice — travelpluscost

> Read this before writing any post or page. The **canonical voice file** — the hard rules + how we
> sound. Companions (kept from the Hawaii system, ~90% universal): `DanKennedyVoice.md` (direct,
> proof-led, one next step), `Humour.md` (the dry-beat standard), `PassAISlopAndDetection.md` (don't
> sound like AI). Replaces the Hawaii-picnic voice; the *craft* is the same, the *persona* is ours.

## Who we are (the persona)
The honest one in a rigged industry. We sell hotels at the hotel's cost plus one small flat fee — the
**same price for everyone, never set from your data.** We talk like a sharp friend who's read the fine
print so you don't have to: plain, specific, a little dry, allergic to marketing nonsense. We explain
*why you pay what you pay in 30 seconds.* If we can't, we don't deserve the booking.

## Hard rules (non-negotiable)
1. **Spell every word OUT — no contractions** (write "it is", "you are", "do not"). Also **no dashes** and **no
   colons or semicolons in prose** — split with `.` and `,` (owner rule 2026-06-28, enforced by `npm run blog:style`).
2. **No exclamation marks. No emoji.** Deadpan beats hype.
3. **No AI tells** — 0 HARD in `ai-slop-check`. (no "seamless / world-class / unlock / nestled / hidden
   gem / turn-key / elevate / in today's world …".)
4. **Plain, high-school words** — *unless* a fancier word is the actual search term (then keep it).
   "junk fee," not "ancillary surcharge." "the same price," not "rate parity."
5. **Active voice, subject first.** "Your phone changes the price," not "The price is changed by your phone."
6. **Tell people the honest downside** — including about us. We're **not always the lowest headline
   price** (opaque discounters can be cheaper, then add fees at checkout). Say so. Honesty *is* the product.
7. **Short paragraphs (max 3 sentences).** Readers skim for the 1–2 facts they need.
8. **Write to CONVERT, not to impress.** Cut filler, keep the beat (see Humour.md). Specific + plain wins.

## What we punch at (and what we never punch at)
- **Fair game:** surveillance pricing, fake discounts, "1 left!" scarcity, junk resort fees, loyalty-point
  mazes, "preferred/VIP" tiers, confusing-by-design checkout, the industry's tricks — and ourselves.
- **Never:** the reader, anyone's budget, or a *specific* competitor by an unprovable claim. Any claim
  about a rival must be **our own reproducible, recorded test** ("here's what I got, same minute") — never
  an accusation we can't show. (Defamation + FTC risk — see POSITIONING.)

## The promise, said our way (use, don't overclaim)
> The same price for you, your mom, and the cousin you haven't seen in 20 years — from any phone, any
> city, any day. We never touch your data to decide what you pay. No fake discounts, no points games, no
> nonsense.

Do **not** stretch it into "lowest price anywhere" or "no dynamic pricing" — we control *our* fee, not the
hotel's base rate (see `stats.md` §2).

## Quick gut-check before shipping
- Would someone who's been burned by a junk-fee checkout nod? (truth + specifics)
- One dry beat per section — a smile every ~200–300 words? (Humour.md)
- Is the single most useful thing findable in 10 seconds? (answer-first, bold, a strip)
- Did I explain the price/point in plain words a 15-year-old gets in 30 seconds?
