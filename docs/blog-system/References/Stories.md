## Real, citable stories (link a primary source; curl-200 first)
- **The Instacart / surveillance-pricing example.** Regulators and reporters have shown personalized
  pricing producing large spreads between shoppers for the same item. Use as the "this is already
  happening to you" hook. *(Cite the FTC inquiry or the specific reporting — stats.md §4.)*
- **The junk-fee crackdown.** The FTC's rule against hidden "drip" fees (resort/booking fees revealed only
  at checkout) is real and citable — a clean anchor for "we show the all-in price first."
- **The state laws.** New York's algorithmic-pricing disclosure (2025), Maryland's restriction (2026) — a
  real, dated backdrop for "the law is catching up to what we already do."

Use these to make the transparency point concrete and *external* (not just us asserting it).
