# Opinions & Hot Takes — travelpluscost

> Real, specific, *honest* opinions — about travel pricing and our own service. They're what make a post
> sound like a person, not generic AI. **Max ONE per post**, and each must be wired to a real fact or
> principle (never a number we can't show). Replaces the Hawaii picnic opinions.

## On pricing (the brand's core)
- **Your phone shouldn't change the price.** A hotel room costs what it costs; the device you booked it
  on is nobody's business. Pricing from your data isn't "personalization," it's a guess at how much you'll
  overpay. *(Anchor: FTC surveillance-pricing inquiry — stats.md §4.)*
- **The lowest headline price is usually a trap.** The number on the search page and the number at
  checkout are different sports. Junk fees, "resort fees," and "taxes & fees" appear at the last screen on
  purpose. *Show the all-in price first or don't bother.*
- **"Member prices" and points programs are a maze built on purpose.** If you need a spreadsheet to figure
  out what you saved, the program is the product and you're the raw material.
- **Fake urgency is an insult.** "1 room left at this price" and countdown timers are pressure, not
  information. A real deadline is fine; a manufactured one treats you like a mark.

## On how we operate (honest about ourselves)
- **We're not always the cheapest, and we'll say so.** Opaque discounters sometimes beat us — then add
  fees, go non-refundable, or gamble on reliability. We sell the *honest* price, not the bait price.
- **Transparency only counts if you can check it.** "Trust us, it's fair" is what everyone says. Open the
  same hotel in another browser, send it to a friend in another state — same number. That's the whole pitch.
- **Boring is a feature.** No spinning "deals," no vanity tiers, no confetti. One price, explained in 30
  seconds. The excitement should be your trip, not our checkout.

## How to use one
Pick the ONE that fits the post's topic, state it plainly in our voice, back it with the linked fact or
the provable principle, then move on. Never stack two. Never soften it into a non-opinion.
